Forecasting 20/20

Predicting and forecasting ETF performance with machine learning

Let's explore some of the different technical indicators or mathematically-based technical analysis tools that are used to analyze the past and predict future price trends and patterns.


Here

Annual Return :

An annual or annualized return is a measure of how much an investment has increased on average each year.

Average Return :

The average return is the simple mathematical average of a series of returns generated over a period of time.

Alpha :

"Alpha" (the Greek letter α) is a term used in investing to describe a strategy's ability to beat the market, or it's "edge."

Beta :

The beta calculation is used to help investors understand whether a stock moves in the same direction as the rest of the market, and how volatile or risky it is compared to the market.



Sharpe :

The Sharpe ratio is calculated by subtracting the risk-free rate from the return of the portfolio and dividing that result by the standard deviation of the portfolio’s excess return.

R-Squared :

The coefficient of determination is a measure used in statistical analysis that assesses how well a model explains and predicts future outcomes.

Standard Deviation :

Standard deviation is a statistical measurement in finance that, when applied to the annual rate of return of an investment, sheds light on the historical volatility of that investment.

Treynor :

The Treynor ratio, also known as the reward-to-volatility ratio, is a performance metric for determining how much excess return was generated for each unit of risk taken on by a portfolio.
